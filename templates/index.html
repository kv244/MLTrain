What is AlphaZero?

AlphaZero is a reinforcement learning algorithm developed by Google DeepMind that masters board games purely through self-play — no human game data required. Starting from random play, the neural network iteratively improves by competing against itself, discovering strategies from scratch. It famously defeated the world's strongest Chess, Shogi, and Go engines within hours of training.

This site applies the same approach to Connect 4: a neural network trained entirely through self-play, evaluated and promoted only when it provably beats its predecessor in a statistically significant match.

Connect 4 Winning Strategies

Control the Centre

The centre column (column 4) connects to the most winning lines. Opening there gives you the highest flexibility for horizontal, vertical, and diagonal threats.

Build Forks

A fork is two simultaneous threats your opponent cannot both block. Force forks by building overlapping diagonal and horizontal setups — the AI excels at this.

Watch the Odd/Even Rule

In Connect 4, who wins a forced endgame depends on whether threats land on odd or even rows. Advanced play exploits this to guarantee a win even when material is equal.

How the MCTS Engine Works

Monte Carlo Tree Search (MCTS) explores the game tree by balancing exploitation (following known good moves) and exploration (trying new lines). Each simulation expands one leaf node, evaluates it with the neural network, and backpropagates the result to update move priorities.

The Think Intensity slider controls how many simulations run per move. At 2000 simulations the engine sees dozens of moves ahead and plays near-perfectly. At 100 simulations it plays quickly but makes occasional tactical errors — great for beginners.

How to Play

  • Two players take turns dropping coloured pieces into a 7×6 grid.
  • Pieces fall to the lowest empty slot in the chosen column.
  • First to connect 4 in a line — horizontal, vertical, or diagonal — wins.
  • If all 42 squares fill up with no winner, the game is a draw.
  • Tip: raise Think Intensity for a stronger (but slower) AI.
